# Style Guide for Jeffrey R. Sonn's Legal Blog Writing

## 1. Voice & Tone
- **Authoritative yet Accessible**: Sonn's writing combines a strong command of legal principles with a focus on making complex regulatory issues understandable for everyday investors.
- **Cautionary and Proactive**: The tone often warns investors about potential risks while encouraging proactive steps to protect their interests.

## 2. Typical Article Structure
1. **Introduction**: Contextualizes the regulatory or enforcement action and its significance.
2. **Regulatory Actions and Findings**: Details specific actions taken by regulators, often with direct quotes or references to official documents.
3. **Investor Implications**: Explains how the regulatory actions impact investors, highlighting potential risks and consequences.
4. **Investor Action Items**: Provides specific recommendations for investors to mitigate risks or pursue recovery.
5. **Conclusion**: Summarizes key points and reinforces the importance of vigilance and informed decision-making.

## 3. Legal Analysis Patterns
- **Regulator Actions**: Sonn often frames regulatory actions as part of a broader enforcement trend, emphasizing the systemic nature of issues like fraud and supervision failures.
- **Contextual Background**: He frequently provides historical context or references past enforcement actions to illustrate recurring patterns and regulatory focus areas.

## 4. Investor Action Item Patterns
- **Review Broker History**: Encourages investors to check regulatory histories to identify red flags.
- **File Claims Promptly**: Advises on procedural steps like filing Proof of Claim in bankruptcy cases.
- **Seek Legal Consultation**: Recommends consulting with legal professionals for personalized advice and potential recovery strategies.

## 5. Words and Phrases to USE
- **"Systemic Failures"**: Used to describe widespread issues within financial institutions.
- **"Regulatory Scrutiny"**: Highlights the focus of regulatory bodies on specific practices.
- **"Investor Protection"**: Emphasizes the primary goal of regulatory actions.
- **"Unsuitable Recommendations"**: Describes inappropriate investment advice.
- **"Supervisory Failures"**: Refers to lapses in oversight by financial firms.

## 6. Words and Phrases to AVOID
- **"Guaranteed Returns"**: Avoids suggesting certainty in investment outcomes.
- **"Best Investment"**: Steers clear of promotional language that implies superiority.
- **"Risk-Free"**: Does not use terms that downplay potential investment risks.
